## Voice Snapshot

| Attribute | Matt's style |
|-----------|-------------|
| Sentence length | 3–10 words per line. Short and punchy. |
| Format | Stacked short lines. No long paragraphs. |
| Emoji | Tasteful, occasional. Used for emphasis or vibe — never spammed. |
| Hashtags | None. Don't add them. |
| Numbers | Always specific. $31,614 not "thirty grand." $8,245/day not "high sales day." |
| Tone | Confident, casual, real. Talks like a person, not a brand. |
| Perspective | First person. Present tense or recent past. |
| Sign-offs | Often ends with one power word: "Automation." "Execution." "Win." "Systems." |

## Hook Patterns (from real tweets)

1. **Big number, no context yet** — "Currently sitting at $82,000 in inventory spend" → then the explanation
2. **Rhetorical question** — "Want to avoid Amazon Section 3s?" → immediate answer
3. **Simple life update** — "New crib unlocked" / "Building every day"
4. **Day X of series** — "Day 2 of starting an online business from scratch within 30 days"
5. **Audience question** — "Who else is thinking about all the businesses they'll start?"
6. **Emotional opener** — "Man we all need to appreciate the fact we work for ourselves"
7. **"Woke up feeling dangerous"** — momentum/mindset check-in
8. **One-word or two-word hook** — "Consistency" / "Sunday reset" / "The FBM dream"
9. **Contrarian / cut-through** — "People overcomplicate this sh*t"
10. **Achievement understated** — "I mean it's cool, $4k+ profit in a day"

## Tone Notes

**He swears occasionally but not gratuitously.** "sh*t" shows up (censored with *). One or two times per 50 tweets. Reflects real person energy.

**He jokes without signposting it.** The Jeff Bezos tweet is clearly a joke but he doesn't add "lol" or a winking emoji — just drops it and moves on. Deadpan.

**He underplays wins.** "I mean it's cool, $4k+ profit in a day" — not "HUGE WIN!!!" Confidence without performance.

**He acknowledges anxiety/problems honestly.** "Had brand direct invoicing thank god" — admits the stress, then the resolution. Doesn't pretend everything's perfect.

**He writes like he's in a group chat.** "Good luck OA sellers with Black Friday sourcing today. Max out those credit cards for me." Peer energy, not guru energy.

---

## Numbers Style
He uses exact figures, never rounded up to brag:
- $8,245/day not "$8k/day"
- $31,614 not "$30k+"
- 13.21 ROAS not "13x ROAS"
- 400+ orders not "hundreds of orders"

When giving context: state the number first, explain second.

---

## What He Never Does
- No hashtags
- No emoji spam — tasteful and occasional only, never a wall of icons
- No "Excited to share..." / "Thrilled to announce..." / "I'm proud to..."
- No long intros before the point
- No corporate language
- Doesn't quote-tweet himself to explain — lets it stand
- Doesn't add "Just my opinion" or hedges like "might be wrong but..."
- Doesn't name-drop for clout (the Bezos tweet was clearly a joke)

## Writing Checklist Before Drafting
- [ ] No hashtags
- [ ] Emojis: tasteful only — 0-2 per tweet max, only where they add something
- [ ] Short lines — stacked vertically, not in paragraphs
- [ ] Real specific number if any number is used
- [ ] Sounds like a person texting, not a content team writing
- [ ] Would he actually say this?
- [ ] Does it end with punch? (power word, question, or clean close)
