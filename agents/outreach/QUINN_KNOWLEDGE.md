## Cold Email That Works
- Short subject lines — no clickbait, no spam words
- Open with a specific result (patient story, number, outcome)
- One clear CTA: book a 15-minute call
- "Confused patient" angle works — patient searched, clicked, never followed up. This is why practices lose leads.
- 5-touch minimum sequence — most replies come on touch 3-4
- Templates: `halo-marketing/outreach/cold-email-templates.md`

## Cold Calling Reality
- 50 calls/day is the floor for SDRs — not optional
- Most prospects don't answer on the first try — it's a volume game

## COLD CALLING — THE INDIRECT APPROACH

Always lead as if you're a potential patient. Get intel on their booking status, then pivot to business. This bypasses gatekeepers and gets you to the doctor.

### SCRIPT 1: "New Patient Inquiry" (Best for gatekeeper bypass)
**Opening:** *"Hey, how's it going? I'm actually calling because I've been dealing with some back issues and a buddy recommended I check out chiropractic care. Are you guys pretty booked up right now or are you taking new patients?"*

**Pivot after they answer:**
*"Oh awesome. Actually, can I ask you something real quick—I'm just curious, how far out are you guys booking? Like, are you slammed or is it pretty easy to get in?"*

**If they say busy/booked:**
*"That's great, sounds like business is good. Actually, that's kind of why I'm calling—I work with chiropractic practices on patient acquisition, and I'm talking to docs who want to stay that busy consistently. Is Dr. [Name] around? I'd love to ask him a quick question about how you guys are handling your new patient flow."*

**If they say slow/easy to get in:**
*"Got it, okay. So here's the thing—I actually work with chiropractic practices on patient acquisition. The reason I ask is because the practices we work with are booking out 2-3 weeks consistently. Is Dr. [Name] available? I'd love to show him what's working—takes like 15 minutes."*

**When you get the doctor:**
*"Dr. [Name], hey—I appreciate you grabbing the phone. So I called asking about appointments just to get a sense of how booked you guys are. Can I ask you, on a scale of 1-10, how happy are you with your new patient flow right now?"*

- **Low score (1-6):** *"Yeah, that's what we're hearing from most practices. It's inconsistent, right? Some weeks are great, some are slow. Listen, we've built this system for chiropractors that makes it way more predictable—consistent new patients every week without you having to think about it. We handle the ads, our call center follows up with every lead and books the appointments directly into your schedule. Would it make sense to jump on a quick call this week? I can show you exactly what we're doing, takes about 15 minutes."*
- **High score (7-10):** *"That's awesome, what are you doing that's working?" [Listen] "Nice. The practices we work with are averaging [X new patients/week]. Even if you're doing well, might be worth 15 minutes to see what they're doing differently—you might pick up something useful. You open to that?"*

## BOOKING THE DISCOVERY CALL

**Standard close (once they're interested):**
*"Cool, so here's what makes sense—let's grab 15 minutes this week. I'll walk you through exactly what we're doing, show you some results from other practices, and you can decide if it's worth exploring. I've got [Tuesday at 2 or Thursday at 10]—which one works better?"*

Key principle: **Give two specific options, not open-ended.** Forces A/B choice, not yes/no.

**Booking objection handlers:**
- *"I need to think about it"* → "What specifically do you need to think about? Maybe I can answer it real quick." → then re-offer the call
- *"Call me back next week"* → "Is it that you're genuinely slammed, or not sure it's worth the time?" If unsure: offer the call now
- *"Not ready to commit"* → "You're not committing to anything—this is just 15 minutes to show you what's working. No obligation."
- *"Need to talk to partner/spouse"* → "Do you want to include them on the call? Might be easier than explaining it all later."

## DISCOVERY CALL OUTLINE

**Duration:** 15-30 min | **Goal:** Understand their practice, identify pain points, position proposal call

### Pre-Call Prep (5 min)
- Website review (services, locations, design quality — note if website is poor, it's a selling point)
- Google Reviews (volume, common complaints)
- LinkedIn (confirm decision-maker)
- Google ranking in their area
- Note obvious marketing gaps

### Opening (2 min)
*"Thanks for taking the time, Dr. [Name]. I've got 15-30 minutes blocked—does that still work?"*
*"Perfect. My goal is simple: learn about your practice, understand your patient flow, and see if we can take that worry off your plate. I'll ask questions, then we'll discuss if there's a fit. Sound good?"*

**Tone: Consultative, not salesy. You're diagnosing.**

### Section 1: Practice Overview (3-4 min)
- "Tell me about your practice—how long have you been operating?"
- "How many locations? Any plans to expand?" *(If yes: "What's holding you back?")*
- "Do you have associate doctors? Is that a goal?"
- "Are you the sole owner or do you have partners?"

### Section 2: Patient Volume & Pricing (5-6 min)
- "Walk me through a typical month—what's your new patient volume?"
- **[CRITICAL]** "Is that consistent or does it fluctuate?" *(If fluctuates: "What causes those swings?")*
- "What's your ideal new patient number per month?"
- "What do you charge for a first visit? And follow-up visits?" *(Need for ROI calc)*
- "What does the full new patient flow look like from the time they book to showing up for their first consultation?"

### Section 3: Mental Bandwidth (2-3 min)
- **[GOLD QUESTION]** "If you never had to worry about where your next patient comes from, what would you focus on instead?"

*(Use their exact words in the proposal call)*

### Section 4: Current Marketing (3-4 min)
- "What are you doing now to bring in new patients?"
- "What's working? What's frustrating you?"
- **[REVEALS LEAKAGE]** "What happens to leads that come in after hours or that your front desk doesn't reach?"
- "Have you worked with marketing agencies before? How'd that go?"

### Section 5: Budget & Decision-Making (3-4 min)
- "What are you currently investing monthly in marketing?"
- "If I showed you a way to bring 20-30 extra patients per month consistently, what would that be worth?" *(Math: 25 patients × $[first visit price] = $X,XXX/month value)*
- **[CRITICAL]** "Are you the person who makes final decisions on marketing, or is anyone else involved?"
  - If not: "Who else? Can we get them on the next call?"

### Section 6: Timeline (1-2 min)
- "If we solve this for you, how soon would you want to start?"
- "Anything else that needs to happen before you could move forward?"

### Closing the Discovery
**Summarize back:** Repeat current volume, ideal volume, pricing, main frustrations (their words), what they'd focus on instead, budget.

**Position proposal call:**
*"Based on everything you've shared, I think we can absolutely take this off your plate. I want to put together a custom plan for [Practice Name]—where we'd find patients, how we'd capture every lead, and how we'd keep your schedule full while you focus on [their priority]. I need about a day to build this properly. Can we schedule 30 minutes on [Thursday/Friday] to walk through it?"*

**Book specific time before hanging up. Within 1-3 days.**

### After the Call
- Update GHL → "Discovery Done" stage
- Notes: volume, goals, pricing, pain points (exact words), budget, decision-maker status
- Send calendar invite immediately
- Send follow-up email with their pain points + goal listed

### Red Flags — Disqualify Fast
- ❌ Not decision-maker + can't get them on next call
- ❌ Budget under $1,500 with no flexibility
- ❌ No clear pain point (they're happy with volume)
- ❌ Unrealistic expectations

**Component 1 — Targeted Lead Generation:**

## OBJECTION HANDLING

### "I need to think about it"
*"Absolutely. Help me understand—when you say you need to think about it, is it the investment amount, the timing, or something about the system itself that doesn't make sense?"*
- Timing → when would work better? Note 2-week onboarding lead time.
- Investment → see ROI objection below
- Don't trust it'll work → reference case study, note 3-month minimum gives time to prove results

### "That's more than I wanted to spend"
*"I hear you. What were you hoping to invest?"* → Then: *"Here's the thing—most agencies charge $5-6K for what we're offering. We're already at half that. But let's look at it differently: if we bring you 25 extra patients at $[their patient value], that's $[revenue] in new revenue vs. $[package price] investment. Does that change how you're thinking about it?"*

If still stuck: *"If the ROI is there—meaning you're making more than you're spending—is it really about the monthly number, or is it about trusting this will work?"*

### "I need to talk to my partner/spouse"
*"That makes total sense. What questions do you think they'll have?"* → Address concerns now. Offer 3-way call: *"Would it make sense to just get them on a quick call with us? That way they hear it directly and you can make the decision today."*

### "I'm already working with someone / trying something else"
*"Got it. How's that going? Are you getting the results you want?"*
- Not happy → "That's exactly why we built this system—to actually deliver predictable results."
- "Waiting to see" → "How long have you been waiting? What's your threshold for pulling the plug?"
- In contract → "When does that end? Let's stay in touch so when it's up, we can jump in."

### "I don't understand the technology / AI stuff"
*"You don't need to understand any of the technology—that's our job. Think of it this way: you have a dedicated team that handles every single lead. They call people, confirm appointments, send reminders. You just see new patients showing up on your calendar. That's it. It's like having extra front desk staff except they never call in sick and work 24/7."*

## Warm Leads vs. Cold Leads — Run Different Plays

**Warm leads (referrals, people you know):**
- They already trust you before the call
- Discovery call can be shorter or skipped
- Second call is just clarifying questions + close
- Don't re-pitch what's already sold — answer questions and ask for the sale
- Andy was "basically already closed" walking into his third call. Matt just answered 3 questions and he signed.

### Price Reveal Sequence (Real Language Used)

**Key pattern:** Always separate ad spend from retainer BEFORE giving the number. Say "ad spend goes to Facebook — doesn't come to us" first, THEN reveal the $1,975. Removes the sticker shock of a $3K+ combined number.

---

### The Two Types of Price Objections (Critical Distinction)

**Objection Type 1 — Confidence ("Will it work?"):**  
→ Fix with a guarantee: "Either we get you X patients in 90 days or we give your money back"  
→ Works when the prospect believes in the results conceptually but needs de-risking

**Objection Type 2 — Liquidity ("I literally can't pay it"):**  
→ Guarantee does NOT work — they literally don't have the cash, not a risk issue  
→ Fix with: half-off first month  
→ Real exchange from this call:

> **Matt:** "What if we give you a guarantee? Either we get you X patients or money back."  
> **Prospect:** "I can't commit to two grand. I just can't. The issue is the actual liquidity."  
> **Matt:** "What if we did the first month for half off — round it up to a thousand dollars?"  
> **Prospect:** "That would be better for sure."

**How to identify which type:** Ask "Even if we could guarantee you'd get those patients or you don't pay — is that still a no?" If yes → it's liquidity. If they soften → it's confidence.

---

### The 3-Month Commitment Reframe

Prospect's exact reaction to hearing it was a 3-month commitment:
> "I have no issue with the 90 days. I thought you were going to say a year. If you would have said any less than 90 days, I would have been like, oh yeah, you're full of crap — because there's no way in hell you're getting results in less time."

**Key insight:** The 3-month minimum is NOT an objection — it's a credibility signal. Sophisticated business owners EXPECT a multi-month commitment. Prospects who push for 1-month trials are actually less serious, not more.

Matt's framing:
> "The reason we have a three-month commitment is that first month, it takes us seven to 14 days to get your systems fully set up. Then it takes about three weeks to ramp the campaigns..."
