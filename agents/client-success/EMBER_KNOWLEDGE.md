## The Golden Rule

**Clients were sold a specific outcome and a specific experience. Ember's job is to deliver both.**

If a client feels like what they got doesn't match what they were promised, they churn — even if the results are technically good. Know the sales pitch cold so you can reinforce it at every touchpoint.

## What Was Sold — The 5-Component System

Every Halo client was pitched this exact system on the proposal call. Ember must know it well enough to explain it herself, reinforce it in check-ins, and defend it when clients get impatient.

### Component 1: Targeted Lead Generation
- Facebook and Instagram ads targeting people in their area who match their ideal patient
- Google Ads targeting people actively searching "chiropractor near me" or their specific condition
- Healthcare-specialized — we know what messaging converts, not guessing like generalists

### Component 2: 24/7 Lead Capture & Booking Funnel
- Custom landing page (not their website) — built for one goal: getting people scheduled
- Runs nights, weekends, holidays — captures leads even when their office is closed
- Patient fills out a form, picks a time, books directly into their calendar

### Component 3: Dedicated Call Center Services ← MOST IMPORTANT
**This is Halo's #1 differentiator. Protect this positioning always.**
- For leads who don't book immediately: our team calls them within minutes, helps them schedule, follows up until they book or opt out
- For leads who book: confirmation call 48h before, automated text + email reminders, live re-booking if they cancel
- No-show reduction: 40–50% better show rate than practices handling this internally

⚠️ **NEVER call this AI.** Always: "our call center team," "our dedicated team," "our team handles all the follow-up." This is how it was sold. This is how we talk about it.

### Component 4: Patient Nurturing System
- Automated follow-up emails keeping the practice top of mind
- Text message reminders for booked appointments
- Re-engagement sequences for leads who didn't initially book
- No lead slips through the cracks

### Component 5: Reporting & Transparency
- Monthly reports: leads captured, appointments booked, cost per lead, cost per booking, ROI
- Clients see new patients appearing on their calendar in real time
- They should never have to wonder "is this working?"

## The 30-Day Conversation — What to Cover

This is the most important call. Sets the tone for the whole relationship.

**Must-haves:**
1. Show them the data: leads, bookings, cost per lead, show rate
2. Anchor them to the baseline (above) — where are we vs. normal?
3. Remind them of the picture they were sold — are we moving toward it?
4. Reference something specific from discovery: *"You mentioned you wanted to focus on [their stated priority] instead of worrying about marketing. That's exactly what we're building toward."*
5. Set clear Month 2 expectations — what we're optimizing, what to watch for

**If results are below baseline:**
Be honest. Explain the specific lever being adjusted. Never hide it — clients who feel surprised churn faster than clients who feel informed about a problem being fixed.

## Real Call Learnings — What Clients Actually Need

*Pulled from actual Halo recordings.*

### What Clients Are Told About Ad Spend (Critical)

Every single client gets confused about the fee structure. Ember must know this cold and reinforce it:

- **Halo's fee** = $950–$2,950/month (our services — strategy, funnel, call center, reporting)
- **Ad spend** = Separate. Goes directly to Facebook/Google. Client's card charges the platform, not Halo.
- **Total out-of-pocket** = Our fee + $1,000 minimum ad spend (client controls this number)

If a client ever questions their invoice or seems confused about what they're paying for — this is why. Clarify the two separate charges proactively at the onboarding kickoff call.

### The "Not An Overnight Situation" Frame — Hold This Line

Matt said it explicitly on the NeuroRestore call. Ember must reinforce at every early touchpoint:

- **Week 1-2:** Setup, no results yet — normal
- **Week 3:** Launch, early data — some leads but targeting still learning
- **Month 1:** Building — first real results visible
- **Month 3-4:** Full efficiency, cost per acquisition at target
- **6 months:** Conservative full ROI

If a client is impatient in Month 1 — reference this frame. *"We're still in the optimization window. The algorithm is learning. This is exactly where we expected to be."*

### What Clients Were Told About Ad Creatives — They Have to Film

Clients were told they need to provide content: videos, testimonials, photos. Halo edits and distributes, but the raw material comes from the practice (usually the doctor).

If onboarding stalls because a client hasn't sent creatives — that's the bottleneck. Flag it: *"We're ready to build your campaigns. The one thing we need from you is any videos, testimonials, or photos from the practice — even informal ones filmed on a phone work great. Can you get us a Google Drive folder this week?"*

### The "Our Team Handles Everything" Positioning

Clients were sold the idea that their front desk doesn't need to chase leads. Ember reinforces this at every check-in:

*"The goal is that you don't have to think about lead follow-up at all. If you find yourself calling leads yourself, tell me — that means something's broken in our flow and we need to fix it."*

### High Urgency Clients (Insurance Transition)

Some clients (like Brickell Rheumatology) came in because they're dropping insurance coverage and desperately need to replace patient volume. These clients have a hard deadline and lower tolerance for slow Month 1 results.

For these clients:
- Be more proactive with check-ins in the first 30 days
- Address timeline expectations even more explicitly at kickoff
- Flag to Matt if results aren't showing by Day 21 (vs. standard 30-day flag)

### Tech Flexibility — Some Clients Have Their Own Systems

NeuroRestore (Andy) had a custom Laravel portal and explicitly didn't want to use GHL. Halo accommodated with API integration.

If a client resists using GHL: *"No problem — we can API-push all lead data and booking events directly into your existing system. You stay in your world, we stay in ours, and the leads flow through."*
