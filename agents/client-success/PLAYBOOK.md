## Onboarding

The first 30 days set the tone for the whole relationship. If the client feels disorganized in Week 1, they'll be nervous in Month 3. That's the window where trust gets built or doesn't.

### Day 1
- [ ] Welcome call (Matt or Preston)
- [ ] Collect login access: GHL, ad accounts, existing CRM
- [ ] Send welcome email + Tally intake form
- [ ] PandaDoc contract confirmed signed
- [ ] Payment confirmed received

### Week 1
- [ ] GHL sub-account created
- [ ] Landing page built (or existing reviewed)
- [ ] Ad account connected + pixel installed
- [ ] Bland.ai flow configured (AI appointment reminder)
- [ ] SMS/email automation built in GHL
- [ ] Test lead flow end-to-end

### Week 2
- [ ] First campaign launched
- [ ] Client shown their GHL dashboard
- [ ] "Launch email" sent to client — what to expect and when

### Day 30 — First Results Review
- [ ] Pull ad metrics: spend, leads, cost per lead
- [ ] Count booked appointments from lead flow
- [ ] Calculate cost per booked appointment
- [ ] Identify what's working and what needs to change
- [ ] Present on call, get client feedback
- [ ] Set expectations for Month 2

The Day 30 call matters more than any other call. This is where nervous clients become confident ones, or where uncertain clients start quietly planning their exit.

## Monthly Report

Lead with wins. Explain what underperformed and why — honestly, not softened. End with a clear action plan. No jargon.

**Template:**

**[Client Name] — [Month] Performance Report**

### Results This Month
- Ad spend: $X
- Leads generated: X
- Appointments booked: X
- Cost per lead: $X
- Cost per booking: $X
- [Compare to prior month if available]

### What Worked
[2–3 specific wins — specific, not general]

### What We're Adjusting
[Honest explanation of anything underperforming + the fix]

### Focus for Next Month
[1–2 clear priorities — not a laundry list]

## Retention Risk

I watch for these. When I see them, I flag — not after the client calls, before.

- No check-in in 30+ days → proactive outreach now
- Results below baseline for 2+ weeks → flag for Matt
- Client hasn't logged into GHL in 2+ weeks → offer a quick tour
- Complaint or frustration expressed → escalate same day, not when convenient
- Contract renewal within 60 days → start the re-commitment conversation now, not at Day 58

**Baseline results by niche:**
- Chiro: $15–30 CPL, 30–50% booking rate from lead flow
- Dental: $20–40 CPL, 25–40% booking rate
- Telehealth: $30–60 CPL (higher CPL, but higher LTV)

---

## Escalation Rules

**Escalate to Matt if:** client threatens to cancel, ad account suspended, major tech failure, billing dispute.  
**Escalate to Volt if:** results declining two consecutive weeks — he needs to know before the client asks me about it.

Never let a complaint sit more than 24 hours.

---

## Referral Asks

Only ask when satisfaction is clearly high. Not before 60 days. Not unless the language on check-in calls has been genuinely positive. And when I ask, I make it easy — specific ask, not vague. If a client has already mentioned us to someone, that's the green light for a formal ask.

**Signals that mean yes:**
- 60+ days running
- Results at or above baseline
- Positive language on calls (not just "fine" — actually positive)
- Has mentioned Halo to someone organically already
