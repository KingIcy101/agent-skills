## Who We're Targeting — The ICP

**Primary niche:** Chiropractors (current focus)
**Secondary (expanding):** Dental practices, telehealth/specialized medicine

### The Ideal Chiropractic Prospect

| Signal | What to Look For |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Practice size** | 1–3 locations, solo owner or small partnership |
| **Years in operation** | 2+ years (they've survived early struggles, now want to grow) |
| **New patient hunger** | Google reviews mention wait times OR reviews are thin (low volume) |
| **Marketing gap** | No visible Facebook/Google ads, outdated website, or missing from local search |
| **Decision-maker accessible** | Owner is the doctor — not a hospital system or large DSO |
| **Geography** | Any US metro or suburban area with population 50K+ |

### Green Flags (Qualify Up)
- Practice has been open 3–10 years (established but still hungry)
- Sparse or old Google reviews → low new patient volume
- Website looks like it was built in 2015 → not investing in marketing
- Google rank is poor for "chiropractor [city]" → organic SEO not working
- No ads visible on Facebook Ad Library → not running paid ads
- Single location, owner-operated → one decision maker, fast close
- Expansion ambitions visible (mentions "future locations" on site, multiple doctors listed)

### Red Flags (Disqualify Fast)
- ❌ Part of a large franchise or hospital system (not the decision maker)
- ❌ Fully booked with a 6+ week waitlist (no pain, no need)
- ❌ Less than 1 year in business (not stable enough, unrealistic expectations)
- ❌ Previous agency horror stories with no openness to try again
- ❌ Budget clearly under $1,500/month (no flexibility signal)
- ❌ Owner not the doctor (office manager runs everything — slow or impossible to close)
- ❌ Multiple negative reviews about billing/scams → problem client risk

## The Pre-Call Research Brief — What to Pull Every Time

Before Quinn or Matt/Preston calls anyone, Scout pulls this for every prospect:

### 1. Practice Overview
- Name, location, years open
- Number of locations
- Estimated number of providers (solo vs. group)
- Owner/doctor name confirmed via website, LinkedIn, or NPI Registry

### 2. Online Presence Check
- **Website:** Modern or outdated? Does it have online booking? Is it mobile-friendly?
- **Google Reviews:** How many? What's the rating? Any patterns in complaints?
- **Google ranking:** Search "chiropractor [city]" — where do they appear? Page 1, 2, buried?
- **Social media:** Active Facebook/Instagram? Last post date?

### 3. Current Marketing Activity
- **Facebook Ad Library** (facebook.com/ads/library): Search practice name + location. Running ads? What kind?
- **Google Ads:** Any ad shows up when you search their name or "chiropractor [city]"?
- If NO visible ads → marketing gap confirmed → strong signal

### 4. Estimated Patient Volume
Based on:
- Review velocity (how many reviews per month, roughly)
- Website booking availability (can you get a same-week appointment? = lower volume)
- Staff size visible on site

### 5. Likely Pain Points (Flag the Most Relevant)
- **Inconsistent flow** → reviews are sporadic, not steady
- **After-hours leakage** → no online booking, no chat widget, no call routing
- **No nurture** → no email capture, no follow-up sequence visible
- **Outdated presence** → website/social not updated in 6+ months
- **Growth ambitions** → mentions expansion, hiring, or second location

### 6. Recommended Discovery Angle
Based on what you found, give the caller one sentence:
> "Lead with the after-hours leakage angle — they have no online booking and their reviews mention phone issues."
> "Lead with inconsistency — reviews are bunched in some months, dead in others."
> "Lead with expansion — their site says they're opening a second location next year."

## The ROI Math — Know This Cold

Quinn and Matt use these numbers on calls. Scout pre-calculates them per prospect.

**Formula:**
```
Monthly value of new patients = (target new patients/month) × (avg first visit value)
ROI multiple = monthly value ÷ retainer price
```

**Typical inputs:**
- Chiro first visit: $100–$200 (ask on discovery call)
- Conservative new patient target: 20–25/month
- Retainer: $1,950 (standard) or $2,950 (premium/multi-location)

**Example:**
- 25 new patients × $150 first visit = $3,750/month new revenue
- vs. $1,950 investment = ~2x ROI on first visit alone
- Add patient LTV (avg chiro patient stays 3–6 months, 2–4 visits/month):
  - $150/visit × 3 visits/month × 4 months = $1,800 LTV per patient
  - 25 patients × $1,800 LTV = $45,000 potential value from one month of leads

**Always use conservative numbers in proposals.** Never promise 40 new patients if you can't back it up.

## Real Call Learnings — What Discovery Actually Reveals

*Pulled from actual Halo recordings.*

### The Insurance-to-Self-Pay Transition = High Urgency Lead

Brickell Rheumatology (Cindy's practice) was transitioning from insurance to no-insurance in March. That meant:
- More than half their current patients were insurance-based and would drop off
- Only 6-7 self-pay patients/month currently
- Desperately needed to replace that patient flow fast

**Scout flag:** If a practice is dropping insurance coverage (or has recently), mark as **HIGH URGENCY**. They need new patients yesterday. Move them to the top of Quinn's list.

### Current Agency with No Funnel = Easiest Pitch in the World

Cindy's exact words: *"We are on the searchers — but we don't have a funnel. Those leads are coming and that's it. There is no follow up, there is no email marketing, there is no tracking. We receive these people and we call them. For some reason they say 'let me think about it.' There is no further plan."*

When a practice already pays for ads but has no CRM, no follow-up, no booking flow — that's the easiest close. The current agency is doing them harm. Note this on the pre-call research brief: **"Existing agency with no lead nurture = walk-in close."**

### No-Show Rate Pain = Unprompted

Cindy brought up no-shows without being asked. This is a universal pain point you should be surfacing on every research brief: "Do they have an appointment reminder system visible? Any reviews mentioning no-shows or last-minute cancellations?"

### Questions to Surface in Pre-Call Research

From the Cindy discovery call — these are the things Matt asked that mattered most:
1. How many patients/day can they realistically see? (capacity ceiling)
2. Are they running Google only, or Facebook too?
3. Do they have a CRM or booking funnel?
4. What happens to after-hours leads?
5. Are they transitioning insurance → self-pay?
6. What's retention like? (If strong, the problem is purely new patients — cleaner pitch)

Add a "lead quality score" field to every research brief based on these signals.

### Multi-Business Prospect Rule

When a prospect has multiple businesses, always:
1. Map all of them in the pre-call brief
2. Note which one has the most immediate patient acquisition pain
3. Start the pitch with the simplest / most contained business
4. Use that win to earn the bigger conversation

Don't try to solve everything at once — it overwhelms and stalls.
