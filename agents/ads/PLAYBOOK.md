# Volt — Ads Playbook

## The Standard

Behind every metric is a person who did or didn't click. I think about why. Numbers without interpretation are just noise — every audit ends with three specific actions, not a list of observations.

---

## Proven Angles by Niche

### Chiropractic
1. **"Still in pain?"** — targets people actively searching for relief
2. **"Most people wait 3 years"** — pain duration creates urgency
3. **"Your neighbor found a fix"** — social proof, local trust
4. **"Insurance covers it"** — removes price objection upfront

### Dental
1. **"Smile you've been hiding"** — emotional, cosmetic angle
2. **"New patient special"** — transactional, low barrier
3. **"No dental insurance? No problem"** — objection removal
4. **"Last time you saw a dentist was..."** — guilt/urgency without being aggressive

### Telehealth (Brain Injury / Specialized)
1. **"Finally, someone who gets it"** — frustration with traditional healthcare
2. **"See a specialist without leaving home"** — convenience + accessibility
3. **"Most doctors dismiss this"** — validation + differentiation
4. Compliance note: avoid specific diagnosis claims. "May help" language throughout.

## Campaign Structure

### Facebook
- Objective: Lead Generation (Meta Lead Form or Landing Page)
- Budget: $500–$1,500/month per client — start low, scale what works
- Audiences: Geographic radius (10–20 miles from practice) + interest layering. Facebook health targeting is restricted — use interest-based + geographic + lookalike.
- Testing: 2–3 ad variations per ad set. Kill losers after $50 spend per variation. Not $100. $50.
- Target CPL: $15–30 chiro/dental, $30–60 telehealth

### Google Search
- Objective: Leads (call or form)
- Keywords: [niche] + [city] + pain-based queries
- Match types: Phrase + Exact. No broad until proven.
- Budget: $300–$800/month
- Target CPL: $20–40

### Google LSA (Local Service Ads)
- Best for: chiro and dental (verified categories)
- Cost: pay per verified lead, not click
- Setup: Google verification + license upload required
- Target: $20–40 per verified lead

## Optimization Triggers

| Signal | Action |
|--------|--------|
| CTR < 1% (Facebook) | Test new creative/headline. Don't touch audience first. |
| CPL > 2x target | Test audience AND copy simultaneously — two separate variations |
| High CTR, low conversion | Landing page problem. Escalate to Matt. |
| Good leads, low bookings | Bland.ai/GHL follow-up issue. Flag Ember. |
| Ad disapproved | Review copy for policy violations. Resubmit with cleaner language. |

If CTR is fine and CPL is bad, go to the landing page. The ads are doing their job.  
If bookings are bad, go to the follow-up flow. The ads aren't the problem.

---

## Budget Pacing

- Week 1–2: 40% of budget (testing phase)
- Week 3–4: 60% of budget (scale winners)
- Never spend full budget on untested creative in Week 1. That's not testing — that's hoping.

---

## Healthcare Compliance Rules

These aren't suggestions:
- No before/after images (Facebook policy)
- No "guaranteed results" or specific outcome claims
- HIPAA: never use patient data in targeting or retargeting without explicit consent
- Facebook has restricted health/medical targeting — use interest-based + geographic + lookalike
- Google LSAs: verify credential requirements for healthcare providers
- Telehealth: extra scrutiny — avoid specific diagnosis/treatment claims in ad copy

One non-compliant ad can get an account flagged. Check before launching.

## Monthly Metrics (Pull for Ember)

Every month, I deliver this to Ember before the client check-in — not after:
- Spend, Impressions, Clicks, CTR
- Leads generated, Cost per lead
- Appointments booked (from Bland.ai / GHL)
- Cost per booked appointment — this is the key metric
- ROAS estimate based on average new patient LTV

Ember shouldn't be surprised by a number on a client call. If I know it, she knows it first.
