5. **Cold Calling** — Highest close rate when you get through. Script: "Hi, is this the owner? I work specifically with chiro practices in [city] — we helped [practice] add 20 new patients last month. I have 15 minutes, could I show you how?" If no: "No problem — can I send a quick email with the case study?"

**Stage 3 ($100K+ MRR): Paid Ads + Brand**

1. Run your own Facebook/Google ads targeting practice owners

## SALES FRAMEWORK — DISCOVERY → CLOSE

### The 5-Step Discovery Call (45 minutes)

**Step 1: Connect & Disarm (5 min)**
- Small talk, thank them for their time
- "Before I tell you anything about us, I want to understand your situation. Is that okay?"

**Step 2: Understand Their World (10 min)**
- "How long have you been in practice?"
- "Where do most of your new patients come from today?"
- "What's working well? What's not?"
- "If you could wave a magic wand — what would your new patient flow look like in 12 months?"

**Step 3: Quantify the Gap (5 min)**
- "So you're at [X patients/mo] and want to be at [Y patients/mo]?"
- "What's a patient worth to your practice on average, over their lifetime?"
- "So closing that gap would be worth roughly [$ amount] to you?"
- Let them say the number. It anchors value.

**Step 4: Present Your Solution (15 min)**
- "Based on what you told me, here's exactly what I'd do..."
- Show the specific strategy for THEIR practice (not generic)
- Share the most relevant case study
- Present pricing as a single recommendation, not a menu

**Step 5: Handle Objections + Close (10 min)**

**Common objections in healthcare niche:**
- *"I've tried ads before and they didn't work"* → "Tell me more about that — what happened? [pause] That's exactly why we include X. Our guarantee means if it doesn't work, you pay nothing."
- *"Your price is too high"* → "Compared to what? If this brings you 20 patients at $2K each, that's $40K/month. We charge $2K. That's a 20:1 return."
- *"I need to think about it"* → "Totally understand. What specifically would you want to think through? [pause] Can we address that right now?"
- *"I need to talk to my partner"* → "Great — can we get them on a quick 15-min call this week so I can answer their questions directly?"

**The Close:**
- "Based on everything we talked about, does this feel like the right move for your practice?"
- If yes: "Great. I have availability to onboard 2 more clients this month. Let's get you started today."
- Send contract + invoice immediately after the call. Don't wait.

### Close Rate Benchmarks:
- Cold outreach → call: 2-5% conversion
- Warm referral → call: 40-60% conversion
- Inbound (content/ads): 20-40% conversion
- Target close rate on calls: 30-40%

## RETENTION & DELIVERY AT SCALE

### The #1 Agency Killer: Churn
If you lose 8% of clients per month, you need to acquire 8% of your base every month just to stay flat. At 50 clients that's 4 new clients/month just to tread water.

**Churn prevention is worth more than acquisition.**

### Retention System:
1. **30-60-90 Day Onboarding** — Most churn happens in the first 90 days. Build a structured onboarding that sets expectations, shows early wins, and creates relationship.
2. **Monthly Reporting** — Use AgencyAnalytics or Google Looker Studio. Automated reports that go out on the 1st of every month. Clients who see numbers stay.
3. **Quarterly Business Reviews (QBRs)** — 45-min video call every 90 days. Review results, set next quarter goals, identify upsell opportunities.
4. **Proactive Communication** — Don't wait for clients to ask "how's it going?" Reach out first. Weekly updates in Slack/email.
5. **Fast Resolution Protocol** — When something goes wrong (it will), respond in <2 hours and lead with a solution. Clients leave agencies because of communication failures, not performance failures.
6. **The Sticky Service Bundle** — Add services that are hard to detangle: ad accounts (they own the history), custom audiences, email lists built in their account. Switching cost is real.

### Delivery Standards at Scale:
- Every client gets an assigned Account Manager when you exceed 15 clients
- SLA: Respond to client messages within 4 business hours
- Campaign changes executed within 24 hours of approval
- Monthly report delivered on the 1st, every month, no exceptions
- Red flag protocol: if a client hasn't logged into the dashboard in 30 days, proactive outreach

### The Marcel Petitpas Profitability Formula (from Jason Swenk interview):
```
Revenue - Pass-through Expenses = Agency Gross Income (AGI)
AGI - Delivery Cost = Gross Profit
Gross Profit / AGI = Delivery Margin

Target: 50-60% delivery margin
Warning zone: <40% delivery margin
Crisis zone: <30% delivery margin
```

**Three levers to improve delivery margin:**
1. Decrease delivery cost (better processes, right-level staff, automation)
2. Increase average billable rate (raise prices or cut time-to-deliver)
3. Improve utilization (ensure staff is billable, not idle)

## PRICING STRATEGY

### The Psychology of Pricing

**Rule 1: You are not your client.** Matt may feel $2,000/mo is expensive. His client makes $150-500K/year from their practice. $2,000/mo is 1-3% of revenue for peace of mind on new patients. That's nothing.

**Rule 2: Price signals quality.** A $500/mo agency scares sophisticated buyers. They've been burned by cheap agencies. $2,000+/mo says "professional."

**Rule 3: The market is anchored, not rational.** Clients compare you to other agencies, not to the cost of doing it themselves. Be aware of market rates (healthcare marketing agencies typically charge $1,500-5,000/mo).

**Rule 4: Raise prices BEFORE you feel comfortable.** The window to raise prices without friction is:
- When you're onboarding a new client (just announce the new price)
- When you have a fresh case study (justify with results)
- When you have a waitlist (scarcity creates leverage)

### Pricing Framework by Stage:

| Stage | Price | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| First 3 clients | $1,497-1,997 | Learning + building case studies |
| Clients 4-10 | $1,997 | Validating the offer at full price |
| Clients 11-25 | $2,497 | Price increase with proven track record |
| Clients 26-50 | $2,997 | Premium positioning, refined offer |
| Clients 50+ | $2,997-$4,997 | Tiered options, specialization premium |

### The Matt Banker Insight (from Jason Swenk podcast):
Put your prices on your website. Why?
- "Making one decision in advance instead of making it on every call"
- Filters out bad-fit clients automatically
- Signals confidence and establishes you as a serious agency
- Attracts clients who see the value, not those trying to negotiate

### Value-Based vs. Service-Based Pricing:

**Service-based (wrong):** "We run your Facebook ads — $1,500/mo"
**Value-based (right):** "We generate 15+ new patient leads per month — $1,997/mo"

The value-based price is anchored to the outcome. The client is thinking "15 patients × $2K lifetime value = $30K in revenue. I'm paying $2K for $30K. Easy yes."

## HEALTHCARE NICHE SPECIFICS

### Why Healthcare is a GREAT Niche for Agencies

1. **Recurring revenue business model** — Practices have ongoing patient needs, not one-time projects. Clients stay 12-24 months on average if you deliver.
2. **Clear ROI** — Patient lifetime value is measurable ($500-3,000+ per patient). You can show a 10-20× return on ad spend.
3. **Underserved by sophisticated marketing** — Most practice owners are clinicians first, marketers never. Low competition from the client side.
4. **High urgency** — Empty appointment slots = lost revenue TODAY. They're motivated to fix it.
5. **Referral-rich network** — Chiropractors know other chiropractors. Dentists know other dentists. One great client = referral gold.

### Chiropractor Specifics
- **Pain point:** Empty slots, inconsistent patient flow, reliance on word-of-mouth
- **Dream outcome:** Predictable new patient flow (15-30/month), full schedule
- **Budget range:** $1,500-3,000/mo comfortable; $3,000-5,000 for larger practices
- **Decision maker:** The owner-doctor (often solo or small practice)
- **Sales cycle:** 1-2 calls, 1-3 days to close
- **Best platforms:** Facebook/Instagram (local targeting), Google (search intent)
- **Seasonal patterns:** January (new year resolutions), spring (sports injuries)
- **Key fear:** "I tried ads and it was a waste of money" — counter with guarantee

### Dentist Specifics
- **Pain point:** High-value procedures (implants, Invisalign, cosmetic) not filling; new patient cost rising
- **Dream outcome:** 30-50+ new patient leads/month, more high-value procedure consultations
- **Budget range:** $2,000-5,000/mo; DSOs can pay $5,000-15,000+
- **Decision maker:** Practice owner or office manager
- **Sales cycle:** Slightly longer — 2-3 calls, 1-2 weeks
- **Best platforms:** Google Ads (high intent), Facebook (awareness + retargeting)
- **Key metrics:** Cost per lead, show rate, procedure conversion rate

### Telehealth Specifics
- **Pain point:** Patient acquisition cost, competition from big platforms (Teladoc, etc.)
- **Dream outcome:** Steady flow of new registered patients in their specialty
- **Budget range:** $2,500-7,500/mo (higher due to national/larger scope)
- **Sales cycle:** Longer — B2B-like, multiple stakeholders
- **Recommendation:** Specialize in one telehealth niche (e.g., mental health telehealth, men's health, chronic care) to differentiate

### Regulatory Considerations
- HIPAA: Don't store or process PHI in marketing platforms. Use forms with HIPAA-compliant tools if collecting health info.
- FTC guidelines: "Results not typical" disclosures on case studies
- Facebook ad policies: Healthcare ads face heightened review — always have compliant landing pages

## TOP MISTAKES THAT KILL AGENCIES

### Mistake 1: Staying Generalist Too Long
"We do marketing for everyone" = you're average at everything, excellent at nothing. Generalists compete on price. Specialists command premium. The healthcare niche is Matt's moat — double down.

### Mistake 2: Underpricing and Never Raising
$950/mo is freelancer pricing. It signals low confidence, attracts price-sensitive clients who complain, and makes it impossible to hire great people. Every 90 days, raise prices for new clients.

### Mistake 3: Being the Bottleneck
If Matt is in every client campaign, every email, every call — the agency cannot scale. The first step to scaling is making yourself replaceable in fulfillment. Document → delegate → systematize.

### Mistake 4: No Sales System
Relying on referrals and hope is not a sales strategy. You need: a daily outreach number, a CRM, a follow-up sequence, and a close script. Without this, growth is random.

### Mistake 5: Confusing Revenue With Profit
Jason Swenk interview with Marcel Petitpas: Many agency owners have no idea what their delivery margin is. Revenue ≠ profit. If you don't know your margins, you can grow yourself into bankruptcy. Track AGI and delivery margin from day 1.

### Mistake 6: Scope Creep Killing Margins
A $2,000/mo client that takes 20 hours/month is profitable. That same client taking 60 hours/month due to out-of-scope requests is a loss. Define scope in the contract. Charge for extras.

### Mistake 7: Ignoring Retention
It's 5× cheaper to retain a client than to acquire a new one. If you're churning 10% of clients per month, you need to run on a treadmill just to stay still. Build retention systems early.

### Mistake 8: Hiring Too Late (or Too Early)
- Too late: You're burning out, quality is suffering, you're losing clients because you can't keep up
- Too early: You hire before the revenue is there to support it, draining cash
- Right time: When you have 8+ clients on retainer and can project 6 months of payroll confidence

### Mistake 9: Not Having Contracts
Every client, written contract. Scope of work. Payment terms. Cancellation policy (90 days minimum). Without this, clients ghost invoices and leave with 30 seconds' notice.

### Mistake 10: Building a Job Instead of a Business
If Matt can't take a 2-week vacation without the agency falling apart, he hasn't built a business — he's built a job. The test Jason Swenk uses: could you leave for 3 months and come back to a growing agency? Build toward that from day 1.
