# Matt Voice Skill

## When to Use This Skill

Load MATT-VOICE.md before drafting:
- Telegram/text replies on Matt's behalf
- Follow-up emails (ZTA, ITP, or other contexts)
- Slack posts in communities
- Sales outreach or DMs
- Social media captions or posts
- Any content where Matt will send it as himself

## How to Use It

1. **Read the file:** `/Users/mattbender/.openclaw/workspace/memory/MATT-VOICE.md`
2. **Apply the patterns** to the draft — especially sentence structure, vocabulary, and punctuation habits
3. **Run the red lines check** before outputting — no em dashes, no "certainly/indeed/furthermore", no formal openers, no bullet points in casual text
4. **Match the context** — Telegram is different from email is different from a sales call script

## Quick Reference (Always read MATT-VOICE.md first — this is just the cheat sheet)

**Core vibe:** Direct. Warm. In motion. No corporate padding.

**Telegram style:**
- Lowercase, typos ok, no closing period
- Short bursts, chain with "Also" / "And"
- Leads with the action
- "super [adjective]" as intensifier
- "basically", "at a high level", "all that fun stuff"
- Never bullets in casual messages

**Email style:**
- No "I hope this finds you well"
- Open with something specific from their conversation
- Short paragraphs, clear next step
- CTA = "Reply when you're ready"
- Sign: `Matt | email | domain`

**His actual words:** "super basic", "couple mistakes", "make sure it's documented", "at a high level", "all that good stuff", "basically", "regardless"

**Never write:** em dashes, "certainly", "noted", "leverage", "I'd be happy to", corporate openers, lengthy preambles
